About Us

 
 
 
 
 

Mental Health Friendly Communities provides an innovative and exciting opportunity for mental health professionals, African American faith leaders, community members, leaders from key community organizations, and consumers and family members who may be experiencing mental health challenges to come together to better understand and embrace the cultural connections between spirituality and mental wellness

Previous Next

MHFC includes and addresses the externalized and internalized stigma around mental illness and how it impacts the mental wellness of the African American community. Two of our signature trainings, “A Bridge Over Troubled Waters, The Spiritual Journey of African Americans from Slave Ships Through The White House” and “I‘m A Winner Campaign” identifes the historical causes and the impact and burden African Americans carry with them and the environmental factors that contribute to poor outcomes and a distrust of health systems.Through a series of workshops, congregational and community engagement the program creates “transformACTIONal” partnerships between faith-based corganizations, mental health providers, County Behaviorial Health Care Departments and the African American community.

We also offer an online directory of health care providers and support groups based in Alameda County that offer faith-based treatments and services meant to treat mental conditions.

The most important evidence-based practice is demonstrated love!

Why Mental Health Friendly Communities?

There is an urgent need to reduce mental health stigma and disparity within the African American community. It is our mission, passion and purpose to continue to fuel the movementin California to amplify and address this urgent need through active and intentional engagement of the African American faith community. Although anyone can develop a mental health problem, African Americans sometimes experience more severe forms of mental health conditions due to unmet needs and other barriers. According to the Health and Human Services Office of Minority Health, African Americans are 20% more likely to experience serious mental health problems than the general population due to social determinant factors and institutional racism.

MHFC embraces culturally congruent practices that have been deemed as community defined or promising for the African American community. We identify and partner with cultural brokers and other subject matter experts to ensure that we remain culturally astute and honoring of all faith and spiritual practices, identifed from within the community, that promote wellness for African American consumers and family members. Faith and spirituality have been widely and historically identified as primary protective factors and wellness tools for many African Americans experiencing personal, family and community challenges

Becoming a Mental Health Friendly Community

Mental Health Friendly Communities for African Americans are formed out of a firm commitment from each participating county’s Behavioral Health Department working in partnership with the local faithh communities to fully engage and support this effort. To be certified as a MHFC, the County’s Behavioral Health System, faith-based organizations and the broader community must meet the following requirements:

  1. Have Senior Pastors, faith leaders, Health Ministries and congregants commit to becoming a “Mental Health Friendly Congregation.”
  2. Complete the required trainings outlined on the back of this brochure.
  3. Be a welcoming community that honors the voices of those with lived experience of mental illness as key partners and creates the resources needed to make culturally responsive clinical referrals.
  4. Develop a Mental Health Ministry to lead the MHFC initiative on an ongoing basis, which includes serving as a resource for those in need and continuing to work in partnership with the greater community.
  5. Fully embrace and commit to “The Ten Commitments of a Mental Health Friendly Community.”
  6. Host regular Mental Wellness Fireside Chats for congregants and their communities. Long term sustainability is vital to achieving and maintaining Mental Health Friendly Communities.
  7. Behavioral Health Care Departments will host “Spirituality 101” in an effort to better understand the role of faith as an important source of support and wellness for consumers and family members.
  8. Community stakeholders, African American faith leaders, County BHC staff and providers will attend together the culturally defined and historically honoring workshop, “A Bridge Over Troubled Waters, The Spiritual Journey of African Americans from Slave Ships Through the White House.”
